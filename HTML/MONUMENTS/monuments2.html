MONUMENTS

Welcome to our exploration of India's magnificent monuments, showcasing the country's rich history and vibrant culture! These architectural wonders have been influenced by various empires over centuries, each leaving their unique mark.

MONUMENTS OF INDIA

Monuments are witnesses of India's past; the monuments of India are also the guardian pillars of India's cultural heritage. The monuments of India have become an inspiration for the future generations.

NORTHERN INDIA

Some of the most unusual and greatest works of art are found in North Indian monuments. They showcase the mastery of artists from a bygone era. North India includes Delhi, Himachal Pradesh, Jammu and Kashmir, Haryana, etc. Monuments of India come in a wide range of styles. Also with combinations of Indian antique architecture and civilizations.

CENTRAL INDIA

Monuments are perhaps centralised and pivoted around the states of Madhya Pradesh and Chattisgarh in central India, which date back to centuries into the pre-Christian era. Central India, with its mystical calling of the wild, makes a perfect heritage monument site, which makes visitors turn admirers to return again and again.

NORTH-EASTERN INDIA

Monuments in North-east India include some of the ancient historical structures in the state including Sikkim, Manipur, Assam, Arunachal Pradesh, Tripura, Nagaland, Meghalya, and Mizroam. These monuments are remnants of history reflecting cultural heritage of the place.

EASTERN INDIA

The monuments of Eastern India embody the essence of the region's cultural tapestry, woven from the diverse threads of its constituent states. The states of West Bengal, Odisha, Bihar, and Jharkhand form the vibrant mosaic of Eastern India, and nestled within them are the majestic monuments of a bygone era, waiting to be discovered.

WESTERN INDIA

The monuments of Western India have a distinctive regional flavour of the various states that comprise the region. The states of Rajasthan, Gujarat, Maharashtra and Goa make up the resplendent landscape of Western India, and lying luxuriantly among them are the various monuments of the past era.

SOUTHERN INDIA

South India houses a myriad number of monuments, encompassing the richest plethora of Hindu temples in India. Be it the UNESCO-recognized World Heritage Sites of Mahabalipuram monuments and Chola temples, or the opulent palaces that reflect the regal history of Nizams of Hyderabad and Wodeyar Dynasty of Mysore.

CHITTORGARH - RAJASTHAN

The Chittorgarh Fort, also known as Chittor Fort, is one of the largest living forts in India and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It was the capital of Mewar and is located in Chittorgarh. The fort spans a hill 180 m (591 ft) high and covers an area of 280 ha (692 acres) above the Berach River valley. It includes 65 historic structures: four palaces, 19 large temples, 20 large water bodies, four memorials, and a few victory towers. In 2013, the Chittor Fort, along with four other Hill Forts of Rajasthan, was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

TAJ MAHAL - AGRA

Taj Mahal, mausoleum complex in Agra, western Uttar Pradesh state, northern India. The Taj Mahal was built by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahān (reigned 1628–58) to immortalize his wife Mumtaz Mahal (“Chosen One of the Palace”), who died in childbirth in 1631, having been the emperor’s inseparable companion since their marriage in 1612.

HAMPI - KARNATAKA

Hampi was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire from 1336 to 1565. It was a fortified city with numerous temples, farms, and markets. By 1500 CE, Hampi was the world's second-largest city, attracting traders from Persia and Portugal. However, the empire was defeated by Muslim sultanates, and Hampi was conquered, pillaged, and destroyed in 1565, leaving it in ruins.

SANCHI STUPA - MADHYA PRADESH

Located at Sanchi Town, Madhya Pradesh, India, this Stupa is the oldest stone structure in India that was built during the Mauryan period. Originally commissioned in the third century BCE by Emperor Ashok this huge hemispherical dome with a height of 12.2816.46 m (54.0 ft) consists of a central chamber where the relics of Lord Buddha are placed.

JAGANNATH TEMPLE - PURI

The Jagannath Temple, situated in Puri, Odisha, is a renowned Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Jagannath. It stands as a symbol of faith and devotion, attracting pilgrims and tourists from around the world. The shrine holds historical and mythological significance, offering a glimpse into ancient Indian traditions and beliefs.

KAMAKHYA TEMPLE - GUWAHATI

The Kamakhya Temple in Guwahati, Assam is a revered center of Tantric practices, dedicated to Goddess Kamakhya. The temple is known for the Ambubachi Mela, a festival celebrating the goddess's menstruation. Built in the 8th-9th century, it has undergone many rebuildings and features a unique local style called Nilachal. It's one of the oldest and most important pilgrimage sites in the Shakta tradition, especially for those from Bengal.
